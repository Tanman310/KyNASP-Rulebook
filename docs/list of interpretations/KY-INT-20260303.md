*The Member Association of Brazil has requested an interpretation with respect to the following wording: “One draw check indicator, audible, tactile and/or visual may be used provided it is not electric or electronic. In particular if the visual one draw check indicator (clicker) as shown in the photos below is authorized in recurve division? to help deaf archers and hearing impaired archers who don ́t ear audible one. It's not electric or electronic.*

## Interpretation

The visual draw check indicator as presented is legal in the recurve division. The presented videos of the draw check indicator in use show a visual flag that appears in the athlete’s line of sight simultaneously as the ‘clicker blade’ is released by drawing the arrow through the blade. The primary signal is a visual flag and it is likely that there will also be some audible sound emitted by the draw check indicator. This is allowed under articles 11.1.4 and 22.1.4 – ‘audible and visual may be used’ since the signals occur simultaneously and are considered to be one draw check. For the avoidance of doubt, more than one draw check indicator would be if there were multiple signals, either audible, tactile and/or visual, that are not received simultaneously.
