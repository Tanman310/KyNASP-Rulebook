# Article 4 Competitive Format
*NASP Tournaments emphasize safety, sportsmanship, and indoor application. If an archer is unable to safely use all equipment and follow range protocols, that archer may be removed from the competition.*

## 4.1. Range Set-Up 

### 4.1.1. 
Target butts will be placed directly on the floor (or factory bases), similar to the height of school butts.

### 4.1.2.
Target faces will be placed on the target butts so that the bottom edge touches the floor or as close to the floor as possible. 

### 4.1.3.
Target faces (paper) will be NASP 80 cm FITA face with 10 scoring rings. 

### 4.1.4.
The target line will be approximately 2 yards or meters from the face of the target butts. 

### 4.1.5.
There will be 10-meter (32’ 10”) and 15-meter (49’ 2”) shooting lines. 

### 4.1.6.
The waiting line will be at least 4-5 yards or meters beyond the 15-meter shooting line. 

### 4.1.7.
A ‘Coach’s Alley’ will be delineated between the waiting line and spectators. Only Coaches with the appropriate wristband or tournament credentials are permitted in Coach’s alley. 

### 4.1.8.
Spectator seating will be placed as close as possible behind the spectator line. 

### 4.1.9.
Shooting lines will support 5-foot-wide shooting lanes accommodating 2 archers per lane, each with a 30” space, one in the left half of the lane, one in the right half of the lane. Each lane will have marks indicating the right and left edges and the center. 

#### 4.1.9.1.
The shooter’s label will indicate with “Left” or “Right” which half of the 5’ wide shooting lane each archer is assigned. 

##### 4.1.9.1.1.
Placement of any items or marks on the floor or target as a reference or aiming point is prohibited. 

##### 4.1.9.1.2.
If an archer is assigned to the “left” or the “right,” they are expected to stand in the center of their assigned 30” space, even if they are on a target without another archer. KENTUCKY: This rule is meant to teach the kids not to crowd the front of their 30” or the back of their 30”. Please teach them to center themselves in their space so they are not crowding. It doesn’t mean all archers' feet will be exactly in the center. Even though we all come in different shapes and sizes, we can be aware of our space and be sure we don’t crowd. 

#### 4.1.9.2.
Archers are to be at their assigned area 15 minutes prior to their flight times, with their equipment ready. 

#### 4.1.9.3.
Shooters occupying a lane will be from different schools where possible. 

#### 4.1.9.4.
Coaches are assigned to a group of lanes for their team. They can move archers within their assigned lanes if needed. Archers must indicate the lane change on their scorecards. Archers may only occupy the same side (L or R) of the lane that was assigned to the team. Each lane should still have archers from two different schools. 

## 4.2. Whistle Signals
NASP whistle commands will be used to operate the range. 

### 4.2.1.
5+ whistles for an emergency 

### 4.2.2.
2 whistles to ‘get bow.’ 

### 4.2.3.
1 whistle to ‘shoot’ 

### 4.2.4.
3 whistles to ‘go get arrows.’ 

## 4.3. Arrow Handling and Movement About the Range
NASP safety rules must be followed.

### 4.3.1.
Archers must walk when moving about the range. 

### 4.3.2.
Archers must have one foot on each side of the shooting line with ‘bows on toes’ before shooting begins. KENTUCKY: Archers should have their bodies centered over the shooting line. Straddling the shooting line with one foot on each side. 

### 4.3.3.
While shooting, the archer must straddle the shooting line with the non-target foot behind the back edge of the shooting line. NO PART of the shooting line will be covered by the archer’s foot. 

### 4.3.4.
Shooter and quiver must remain in each archer’s half of the assigned lane when on the shooting line. No part of the archers’ body or the quiver may cover, or be past, their lane marks. 

#### 4.3.4.1.
Crowding across the middle of the lane, or into an adjacent lane, is unsportsmanlike conduct. 

#### 4.3.4.2.
Crowding is defined as any part of the archer’s body or equipment extending past the 30-inch area assigned to the archer. 

#### 4.3.4.3.
The tournament provided arrow quiver must be centered on the shooting line in front of the archer and within 30-inch area assigned to the archer. 

### 4.3.5.
While both archers may approach the target when scoring, only one archer may remain at the targets when arrows are pulled. The other archer must be safely behind the target line, waiting their turn to pull arrows.  

### 4.3.6.
Archers must remain on their feet (standing or squatting) and off their knees when scoring and pulling arrows. 

### 4.3.7.
Arrows are to be pulled one at a time, each placed on the floor, until all are pulled. 

### 4.3.8.
If, after arrows are pulled and the target needs straightening, it is the archer’s responsibility to do so while at the target. Once the archers leave the target, they are not permitted back down the range until after the following end. 

### 4.3.9.
Arrow points must be covered with one hand, and shafts grasped below the vanes with the other hand when walking with arrows. KENTUCKY: All archers will carry arrows with two hands as described. 

## 4.4. Order of Shooting

### 4.4.1.
The archer must nock, pre-draw, draw, and aim in a manner that keeps the arrow pointed safely towards the target throughout the process. (Below the top of the backstop curtain, and away from the floor). KENTUCKY: All archers will follow the 11-step process described. Starting the draw before the arrow is pointed safely toward the target is prohibited. 

### 4.4.2.
Archers must raise the bow from their toes and keep the bow vertical while nocking. 

### 4.4.3.
Archers will shoot one practice end of 5 arrows and 3 scoring ends of 5 arrows at 10 meters. 

### 4.4.4.
Archers will shoot one practice end of 5 arrows and 3 scoring ends of 5 arrows at 15 meters. 

### 4.4.5.
Archers will have 2 minutes to shoot each 5-arrow end. KENTUCKY: Coaches/Archers/Parents are responsible for following and understanding the rules. If an archer is taking too much time, the coach should be instructed by a lane official to remind their archer of the rule. If the rule continues to be violated, an announcer may announce “2 minutes” at the 2-minute mark. If the same archer(s) continue to shoot past the 2-minute mark, the announcer may blow emergency whistles to stop shooting at 2:10. All arrows not shot will be scored as zeros. Non-compliance with the rule may cause the disqualification of the archer. 

### 4.4.6.
Dropped arrows will be left on the floor and replaced by a range official. 

### 4.4.7.
An arrow that bounces out or fails to stick in the scoring area of the target face may be shot again as instructed by range officials. Except for practice ends. 

### 4.4.8.
Replacement arrows will only be provided for bounce-outs on scoring ends. Practice bounce outs will remain as shot. KENTUCKY: If an arrow continues to bounce out, the archer’s draw weight and draw length may not be sufficient to perform on par with the other archers. (rule 4.4.9) Unless there is an equipment issue, each arrow of the end bouncing out can be replaced twice. If the arrow’s third bounce out still does not stick, it will be scored zero points, and the archer will start shooting the next arrow in their end of arrows. 

### 4.4.9.
An arrow that crosses the target line is considered a shot arrow. 

### 4.4.10.
If the archer’s draw weight and draw length are not sufficient to perform on par with other archers in the tournament, the archer may be removed. Shot arrows that fail to reach or stick in the target butts are symptomatic of insufficient performance. 

### 4.4.11.
After shooting the last arrow, the archer must immediately leave the shooting line, rack the bow, and return behind the waiting line. This is a NASP-specific range management protocol. 

### 4.4.11.
If an archer fails to shoot all 5 arrows and returns behind the waiting line, the remaining arrows will be scored as zeros. 

### 4.4.12.
If an archer experiences an equipment malfunction during the shooting process, such as a lost vane, damaged nock, damaged arrow rest, or failure, yet the arrow makes it to the target line and doesn’t bounce off the target, it is a shot arrow. 

### 4.4.13.
If, after returning to the waiting area, an archer’s scorable arrow falls out of the target, it is a bounce out, and a replacement arrow will be given. An archer is to notify a lane official immediately and prior to returning to the shooting line 

### 4.4.14.
If, during a tournament, there is a delay in the shooting that exceeds 10 minutes, the tournament director has the discretion to allow the archers to shoot 5 practice arrows prior to restarting the scoring ends. Examples of a delay could be, but are not limited to power outage, a fire alarm, or a medical emergency. 

## 4.5 Coaching

### 4.5.1.
A team is limited to 3 coaches in Coaches Alley during the flight. Individual groups may have 1 coach in Coaches Alley. 

#### 4.5.1.1.
Any coach in Coaches Alley must have a coach’s wristband or tournament credentials identifying them as the appropriate NASP coach. 

#### 4.5.1.2.
In instances where individual or team groups are split or separated, schools may request one additional coach’s wristband. 

#### 4.5.1.3.
At least one coach must be immediately available and located with their team lanes while the team is shooting, in case they are needed. Coaches must display positive sportsmanship while in this alley. 

### 4.5.2.
Coaches of special needs archers may be allowed (with prior approval of range officials) to stand with the archer on the shooting line. However, they must do so safely and without distracting the other archers. 

### 4.5.3.
Coaches must withhold coaching and communication with their archer while the archer is on the shooting line. Such communication may only take place when the archer is behind the waiting line. 

### 4.5.4.
We encourage coaches to be seated as much as possible to prevent blocking the view of spectators in the audience.
