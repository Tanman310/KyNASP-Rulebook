# Article 1 NASP Participation

<p class="center-italic">To maximize the number of students who will discover their aptitude and interest for archery, NASP is an in-school program. NASP emphasizes team participation to maximize the number of students who will participate and to promote positive social interaction among students and faculty.</p>

**NASP officials reserve the right to make on-site determination of any situation not covered by these rules. NASP officials’ decisions are final.**

## 1.1. Definition of an active NASP School
A NASP school is one that teaches NASP in school as a part of the normal school day and adheres to the training, equipment, safety requirements, and operating principles established by NASP. NASP safety protocols must be followed by all NASP schools and teams to preserve the experience for current and future students.

### 1.1.1.
School lessons must be presented by NASP-certified faculty or volunteers.

### 1.1.2.
At least one coach on the team must be NASP-certified.

#### 1.1.2.1 Kentucky Addendum
All coaches must be NASP BAI certified.

### 1.1.3.
Only NASP-certified teachers or coaches may register student archers for tournaments.

### 1.1.4.
Equipment requirements and range protocols must be followed.

#### 1.1.4.1.
Only the standard Genesis bow & Easton 1820 arrows may be used during NASP class or on a NASP range.

#### 1.1.4.2.
NASP range specifications and design must follow the training provided in the NASP Basic Archery Instructor’s course.

#### 1.1.4.3.
Failure to follow these protocols will make the school ineligible to participate in NASP tournaments. Failure to follow safety protocols, such as bow-and-arrow use and curtain use, may also result in the decertification of the NASP-certified teacher or coach.

## 1.2. In School Requirement
Every archer must attend (be enrolled in) an active NASP school. A school that conducts NASP lessons during normal school hours in accordance with the NASP operating principles.

### 1.2.1.
The student’s primary school of enrollment, where they receive the majority of their in-school educational hours, determines the student’s “school.”

### 1.2.2.
Students must be enrolled in an active NASP school to participate in NASP.

### 1.2.3.
NASP lessons must be conducted as part of the in-school curriculum, a minimum of 10 hours per school, per year.

### 1.2.4.
In-school lessons are the priority of NASP. Ideally, these lessons should be thorough enough for students to enjoy a safe, motivating experience. 

### 1.2.5.
Classroom lessons must include at least the following subject material. 

#### 1.2.5.1.
Determining eye dominance. 

#### 1.2.5.2.
Stringbow used to teach Eleven Steps to Archery Success. 

#### 1.2.5.3.
Safety Orientation and shooting of bows and arrows on a NASP range.

### 1.2.6.
Examples of how this might occur include: 

#### 1.2.6.1.
At least 10 different archery classes of 1 hour each – daily for 2 weeks, twice weekly for 5 weeks, etc. 

#### 1.2.6.1.
5 classes per day, each 1 hour, for 2 different days.

### 1.2.7.
When a school agrees to meet, meet, or exceed the requirements mentioned above, each school year, even if not every student in the school takes the archery class, EVERY student in the school is considered eligible for NASP tournaments. It is expected that even those who do not take in-school instruction will receive safety and shooting form instruction if they join the FOLLOW-UP archery club or team before attending a NASP tournament. 

### 1.2.8.
Participation in NASP tournaments is at the discretion of the school Administration. The school must be aware of and approve of students participating in NASP tournaments. 

### 1.2.9.
Prior to participating in any NASP competitions during a school year, the head coach for a school’s team (or other coaches associated with the school for tournament purposes) will be required to log in to the School Manager for the school and acknowledge that they understand the in-school teaching requirements and commit to making sure all those requirements are met. They must also report hours and student numbers annually. 

#### 1.2.9.1. Kentucky Addendum
Failure to meet the commitment once acknowledged may result in the termination of the BAI and the team’s ineligibility to compete the next year. 

### 1.2.10.
After-school-only archery programs or clubs are not eligible for any NASP events, nor can they be considered a NASP program. 

## 1.3. Divisions within NASP Schools
NASP divisions are the Elementary School division (grades 4 and 5), the Middle School division (grades 6, 7, and 8), and the High School division (grades 9, 10, 11, and 12).

### 1.3.1.
If schools have similar names but are at separate addresses, such as the ‘North Middle School and North Elementary School,’  even if in the same district, they are separate schools and may not mingle shooters on teams.

#### 1.3.1.1.
Except: If elementary grades (4th & 5th) are not in the same school, the schools where the two grades are found may combine students to form an elementary school team if both are NASP schools. Example: this could be a school with K-4 and a combined 5-8 or 5-12 school.

#### 1.3.1.2.
Except: If middle school grades (6-8) are not in the same school, the schools where the three grades are found may combine students to form a middle school team if both are NASP schools. Example: this could be a K-6 school combining with a 7-8 or 7-12 school.

#### 1.3.1.3.
Except: If high school grades (9-12) are not in the same school, the schools where the four grades are found may combine students to form a high school team if both are NASP schools. Example: this could be a school with 9-10, combining with an 11-12 school.

#### 1.3.1.4.
Where divisions are split between schools, the enrollment numbers of both schools apply to the eligibility of the small school rule.

#### 1.3.1.5.
When forming a competitive team for any NASP division, schools must include grades of enrollment within the division they intend to compete in.

## 1.4. Homeschool
A student who is attending homeschool may participate in the following ways.

### 1.4.1.
A homeschool, homeschool group, or homeschool co-op may have individuals trained as BAI’s and request to be an active NASP program in their state, through their state NASP Coordinator.

#### 1.4.1.1. Kentucky Addendum
Homeschools wishing to participate in KyNASP need to contact the Kentucky NASP State Coordinator at lisa.johnson@ky.gov. Be prepared to submit a copy of their “letter of intent to homeschool” that was sent to their local Superintendent’s office as verification of their homeschool status.

### 1.4.2.
If an established (Formal) homeschool group or Co-op that comes together in one location to meet all or part of their required educational lessons to a group of homeschool students has enrolled in their state NASP and provides in-school NASP archery lessons during normal educational hours, by a NASP certified instructor, and meets and reports their numbers according to the requirements of sections 1.1. and 1.2. above, they may form a team in accordance with the standard operating principles outlined in sections 1.1. and 1.2. above. The same standard required of all schools is to have an active NASP program.

### 1.4.3.
If an established (formal) Homeschool group or co-op wants to join with another group utilizing the small school rule, both must be active NASP programs, and they must submit a request to their state NASP Coordinator for approval.

### 1.4.4.
A homeschool student, virtual school student, or online student may participate with their zoned public school that they would normally be enrolled in, with the approval of the school administration.

### 1.4.5.
A homeschool, virtual, or on-line student requests to be a part of their local school NASP and the administration of their zoned public school denies their participation, then an adult must be associated with the school they are attending, become certified as a BAI and offer a minimum of 10 hours of NASP instruction to that student during regular educational hours and report that training annually. Once the BAI agrees to provide this annual training, the student may participate as an individual or join a Co-op as outlined in 1.4.2. 

## 1.5. Vocational, Career, early College, or Technical schools

### 1.5.1.
If the students attending these schools are from an active NASP high school, remain enrolled in their high school of origin, and graduate from that original school, then they may participate with that school.

### 1.5.2.
If the students attending these schools are no longer enrolled in their school of origin and will not graduate from that high school but from the school they are now attending, then the school they are currently attending/enrolled in must be an active NASP school for them to participate.

## 1.6. Eligible Grades
Only students who are in grades 4-12 may participate. 

### 1.6.1. 
A student at any NASP school who has graduated early during the current school year may, at the discretion of the school Principal, participate.

### 1.6.2.
A student who no longer attends an active NASP school due to having moved, being promoted, etc., is ineligible to participate until/unless the student’s new school joins NASP and may then only represent their NEW school. Except for situations described in sections 1.4.3. and 1.4.4. 

## 1.7. Number of Archers Per Team
NASP archery teams must contain 12-24 students, at least 4 of which must be of the opposite gender.

## 1.8. Single-Gender School
If a school is a single-gender school, it shall compete as a single-gender-only school unless eligible to combine with another school under the small school rule (section 1.8). The coach should contact the State Coordinator for their state to set the single-gender indicator for the school. Once set, the team score for the school/division is determined by summing the top 12 scores for the team with no consideration of gender.

## 1.9. Small School
If a competitive division within a NASP school (Elementary 4th – 5th, Middle 6th – 8th or High 9th – 12th) has a total student enrollment of less than 150 students, the team for that division may add students from other competitive divisions within their own school or from one other eligible school/division, in the same city, or county, or school district, or one adjacent County. 

#### 1.9.0.1. Kentucky Addendum
Small schools with multiple divisions will first combine divisions until enrollment of 150 is reached to create a single 12- to 24-member team. If they are a single division school, two small schools may combine to create a single 12- to 24-member team.

### 1.9.1.
Any school that meets the less than 150 student total enrolment per division (Elementary, Middle, High) will first submit the school names, of the schools involved, and the total enrollment numbers per NASP division for the divisions or school that they plan to combine with, to their state NASP Coordinator for approval.

### 1.9.2.
Small schools may draw from a nearby active NASP school even if that school is over the 150-student threshold.

#### 1.9.2.1. Kentucky Addendum
Only two small schools may combine.

### 1.9.3.
A competitive division within a school with 150 students (or more) enrolled may not add archers to their team from another school or division.

### 1.9.4.
If a student shoots up for a higher division team (as allowed by 1.9.), that student will be competing for team rank in that older division but retain individual rank according to the archer’s actual grade.

### 1.9.5.
Unless utilizing this “small school rule” or 1.4.3. and 1.4.4., students may only shoot on a team at the NASP school they attend (are enrolled in).

### 1.9.6.
A request to “shoot-up” must be submitted using the Small School Request screen in School Manager in advance to the state/provincial Coordinator schools involved to be approved.

## 1.10. NASP is an in-school program
If a school administration deems a student unsuited to represent the school, NASP will abide by the decision.

## 1.11.
If a student archer is disqualified for any reason at a provincial, state, or regional tournament, the student is ineligible to participate in any NASP competition until such time as the student has regained good standing in his or her state or province. The NASP coordinator would alert the national office of such a disqualification. 

## 1.12.
Any request to “shoot up” or combine with another school in accordance with Section 1.9. must be done prior to the school’s first team tournament of the year. Once registered in a tournament, that setting may not change for the duration of the season.
