# 1. **NASP Participation**

*To maximize the number of students who will discover their aptitude and interest for archery, NASP is an in-school program. NASP emphasizes team participation to maximize the number of students who will participate and to promote positive social interaction among students and faculty.*

***NASP officials reserve the right to make on-site determination of any situation not covered by these rules. NASP officials’ decisions are final.*** 

## 1. Definition of an active NASP School

A NASP school is one that teaches NASP in school as a part of the normal school day and adheres to the training, equipment, safety requirements, and operating principles established by NASP. NASP safety protocols must be followed by all NASP schools and teams to preserve the experience for current and future students.

#### 1.1

School lessons must be presented by NASP-certified faculty or volunteers.

#### 1.2

At least one coach on the team must be NASP-certified.

#### 1.2.1-KyAdd.

All coaches must be NASP BAI certified.

#### 1.3

Only NASP-certified teachers or coaches may register student archers for tournaments.

#### 1.4

Equipment requirements and range protocols must be followed.
