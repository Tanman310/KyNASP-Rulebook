# Preamble
<p class="center-bold">The Archery Way</p>

<p class="center-italic">An archer pursues excellence with principled sportsmanship. They persevere, show grace when challenged, and stay humble in success.</p>


The **National Archery in the Schools Program (NASP)** is an international, 501 (c) (3) non-profit educational foundation aimed at improving educational performance among students in grades 4–12. NASP promotes and facilitates the instruction of international-style target archery as part of the in-school curriculum. 

NASP uses a consistent model for instruction, equipment, and range management. This allows students across schools, states, and countries to share a common experience. The standards help maintain archery’s strong safety record. They also ensure students face a familiar and fair environment, whether in the classroom, during practice, or at competitions. The NASP model is centered on inclusivity and suited to youth of all genders, sizes, abilities, and economic backgrounds.

This rulebook defines the rules for NASP activities and competitions. It establishes who can participate, what equipment to use, how to run ranges and competitions, and the expectations for sportsmanship. 

NASP officials have the authority to interpret and apply these rules. They resolve situations not specifically covered in the rulebook. By participating in NASP, schools, instructors, coaches, and students accept their responsibility. They agree to uphold the values of safety, honesty, and respect that define the program and the Archer’s Way.
