# Article II - Eligibility and Participation

## 2.1. School Participation

### 2.1.1. Definition of an Active NASP School
An Active NASP School is a school that teaches NASP archery as part of the normal school day and adheres to the training, equipment, safety requirements, and operating principles established by NASP. NASP safety protocols must be followed by all NASP schools and teams.

#### 2.1.1.1.
Instruction shall be conducted by NASP-certified faculty, instructors, or volunteers.

#### 2.1.1.2.
At least one coach associated with the school’s archery team shall be NASP-certified.

#### 2.1.1.3.
Only NASP-certified teachers or coaches may register student archers for NASP competitions.

#### 2.1.1.4.
NASP equipment requirements and range protocols shall be followed during instruction and practice.

### 2.1.2. In-School Requirement
NASP is an in-school program. Every participating archer shall be enrolled in an Active NASP School that conducts NASP lessons during normal school hours in accordance with NASP operating principles.

#### 2.1.2.1.
NASP lessons shall be conducted as part of the in-school curriculum for a minimum of ten (10) hours per school year.

#### 2.1.2.2.
When a school meets or exceeds the instructional requirements described above during a school year, all students enrolled in that school shall be considered eligible to participate in NASP competitions, provided they receive appropriate safety and shooting instruction prior to competition.

#### 2.1.2.3.
After-school-only archery programs or clubs shall not be considered NASP programs and are not eligible for NASP competitions.

### 2.1.3. School Administration Approval
Participation in NASP competitions shall occur with the knowledge and approval of the participating school’s administration.

### 2.1.4.
Participating schools shall operate their NASP programs under the administration of the recognized State NASP Program for their jurisdiction. Where no State NASP Program exists, schools may participate under the authority of NASP or another designated NASP administrative authority.

## 2.2. Student Eligibility

### 2.2.1. 
Students participating in NASP activities or competitions shall be enrolled in an Active NASP School.

### 2.2.2.
The student’s school of primary enrollment, where the student receives the majority of their educational instruction, shall determine the NASP school the student represents.

### 2.2.3.
Only students enrolled in grades four (4) through twelve (12) shall be eligible to participate in NASP activities and competitions.

### 2.2.4.
A student who graduates early during the current school year may, at the discretion of the school principal or administration, remain eligible to participate for the remainder of that school year.

### 2.2.5.
A student who is no longer enrolled in an Active NASP School due to transfer, relocation, promotion, or other circumstances shall be ineligible to participate until the student is enrolled in another Active NASP School and may thereafter only represent the school in which the student is newly enrolled.

### 2.2.6.
If a school administration determines that a student is not suited to represent the school in NASP activities or competitions, NASP shall recognize and abide by that determination.

### 2.2.7.
A student who has been disqualified from participation at a local, state, provincial, or regional NASP competition shall remain ineligible to participate in NASP competitions until the student has been restored to good standing by the appropriate State NASP Program or governing authority.

## 2.3. School Divisions

### 2.3.1.
NASP schools shall compete in divisions based on the grade levels of enrolled students.

### 2.3.2.
The following divisions are recognized within NASP:

* Elementary School Division — Grades four (4) and five (5).

* Middle School Division — Grades six (6), seven (7), and eight (8).

* High School Division — Grades nine (9), ten (10), eleven (11), and twelve (12).

### 2.3.3.
Schools that share similar names but operate at separate locations or addresses shall be considered separate schools and may not combine students to form a team, except as otherwise permitted under NASP rules.

### 2.3.4.
Where grade divisions are split between schools, schools may combine students to form a team within a division when permitted under NASP rules governing combined teams or small schools.

### 2.3.5.
When forming a competitive team for any NASP division, participating schools shall include only students whose grade levels fall within the division in which the team intends to compete.

## 2.4. Special School Types

### 2.4.1. Homeschool
A student who is attending homeschool may participate though the following ways.

#### 2.4.1.1.
A homeschool, homeschool group, or homeschool cooperative may participate in NASP by establishing an Active NASP Program administered by a NASP-certified instructor and approved by the State NASP Program for the jurisdiction.

#### 2.4.1.2.
A homeschool group or cooperative that conducts educational instruction in a shared location may form a NASP team if it provides NASP instruction during normal educational hours, meets the instructional and reporting requirements of an Active NASP School, and is administered by a NASP-certified instructor.

#### 2.4.1.3.
Homeschool groups or cooperatives seeking to combine with another eligible school or homeschool group under applicable team formation rules shall submit a request to the State NASP Program for approval

#### 2.4.1.4.
A homeschool, virtual school, or online student may participate through the public school the student would normally attend, subject to the approval of that school’s administration.

#### 2.4.1.5.
If a homeschool, virtual, or online student requests participation through the local public school and that request is denied by the school administration, the student may participate individually if a NASP-certified instructor provides the required NASP instruction during normal educational hours and reports that instruction annually in accordance with NASP requirements.

### 2.4.2. Vocational, Career, Early College, or Technical Schools

#### 2.4.2.1.
A student attending a vocational, career, early college, or technical school may participate with the student’s originating high school if the student remains enrolled in that school and will graduate from that school.

#### 2.4.2.2.
If a student attending such a school is no longer enrolled in the originating high school and will graduate from the vocational, career, early college, or technical school, that school must be an Active NASP School for the student to participate.

## 2.5. Team Composition

### 2.5.1.
NASP teams shall represent a participating school and shall be composed of eligible students enrolled at that school, except where otherwise permitted under NASP rules.

### 2.5.2.
A NASP team shall consist of a minimum of twelve (12) and a maximum of twenty-four (24) student archers.

### 2.5.3.
A team shall include at least four (4) archers of the opposite gender.

### 2.5.4.
The official team score shall be determined by the combined scores of the twelve (12) highest-scoring archers from the team, provided the team includes at least four (4) archers of each gender.

### 2.5.5.
Schools designated as single-gender schools shall compete as single-gender teams unless otherwise permitted under NASP rules governing combined teams or small schools.

### 2.5.6.
A student archer’s score may be counted toward only one team in a competition. The same score shall also be used to determine the archer’s individual ranking.

## 2.6. Small School Rule

### 2.6.1.
If a competitive division within a NASP school has a total student enrollment of fewer than one hundred fifty (150) students, the team for that division may include students from other competitive divisions within the same school or from one other eligible school division, as permitted under NASP rules.

### 2.6.2.
Schools seeking to combine students under this rule shall submit the names of the schools involved and the enrollment numbers for the applicable divisions to the State NASP Program for approval prior to competition.

### 2.6.3.
Schools combining students under this rule must be located within the same city, county, school district, or within one adjacent county, unless otherwise authorized by the State NASP Program.

### 2.6.4.
A competitive division within a school with one hundred fifty (150) or more students enrolled may not add archers from another school or division.

### 2.6.5.
A student who participates on a team in a higher division under this rule shall compete for team placement in that division but shall retain individual ranking based on the student’s actual grade level.

### 2.6.6.
Requests to combine schools or to permit students to compete in a higher division under this rule shall be submitted for approval prior to the school’s first team competition of the season. Once a school has entered a competition with a team configuration under this rule, the configuration shall remain unchanged for the remainder of the season.

### 2.6.7.
Except as permitted under this rule or under other provisions governing special school participation, students shall compete only for the NASP school in which they are enrolled.

## 2.7. Student Ineligibility

### 2.7.1.
NASP is an in-school program. If a school administration determines that a student is not suited to represent the school in NASP activities or competitions, NASP shall recognize and abide by that determination.

### 2.7.2.
A student archer who has been disqualified at a local, state, provincial, or regional NASP competition shall be ineligible to participate in NASP competitions until the student has been restored to good standing by the appropriate State NASP Program or governing authority.

### 2.7.3.
The State NASP Program shall notify NASP of any disqualification affecting a student’s eligibility to participate in NASP competitions.
