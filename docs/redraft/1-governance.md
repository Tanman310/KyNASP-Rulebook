# Article I - Governance

## 1.1. Name

### 1.1.1.
The name is the National Archery in the Schools Program, abbreviated as NASP, and hereinafter referred to as "NASP."

## 1.2. Status

### 1.2.1.
NASP is a non-profit educational organization under section 501 (c) (3) of the United States Internal Revenue Code.

## 1.3. Objectives

### 1.3.1.
The objectives of NASP shall be:

* To promote and facilitate instruction in international-style archery as part of the in-school curriculum for students in grades 4 through 12.

* To provide and maintain a standardized archery curriculum suitable for use in schools and aligned with applicable educational standards.

* To provide standardized training and certification for teachers, instructors, and coaches responsible for delivering NASP instruction.

* To establish and maintain equipment and range standards that ensure safety, fairness, reliability, and accessibility for all participants.

* To encourage broad student participation in archery and promote educational engagement through the program.

* To ensure that NASP activities are conducted without discrimination on the basis of race, color, sex, sexual orientation, language, religion, political or other opinion, national or social origin, property, birth, or other status.

* To promote a safe, respectful, and harassment-free environment for all participants, instructors, officials, and spectators involved in NASP activities.

* To protect the integrity of NASP competitions by opposing and addressing any form of manipulation of competitions or related corruption.

## 1.4. Program Structure

### 1.4.1.
NASP shall serve as the national governing authority for the program and shall establish the rules, standards, and policies governing NASP activities.

### 1.4.2.
Administration of the program shall be carried out through recognized State NASP Programs operating within their respective jurisdictions and in accordance with the rules and standards established by NASP.

### 1.4.3.
Each State NASP Program shall be administered by a State Coordinator or other designated authority responsible for implementing NASP policies, supporting participating schools, and overseeing NASP activities within that jurisdiction.

### 1.4.4.
Schools that implement the NASP curriculum in accordance with program standards may participate in NASP activities and competitions as recognized NASP schools.

#### 1.4.4.1.
State NASP Programs shall operate in accordance with NASP rules and policies. State Coordinators may establish additional rules or procedures applicable within their jurisdiction, provided such rules expand upon and do not contradict national NASP rules. Any such rules shall be published and clearly identified as State rules or State addenda.

#### 1.4.4.2.
State Coordinators shall administer state tournaments and qualifying events within their jurisdiction and may establish the qualification procedures and score criteria required for participation in state-level competitions.

### 1.4.5.
Instruction and team supervision within participating schools shall be conducted by individuals who have successfully completed NASP instructor certification or other training recognized by NASP.

### 1.4.6.
Students enrolled in participating schools may take part in NASP instruction and competitions in accordance with the rules governing eligibility and participation.

## 1.5. Powers of NASP

### 1.5.1.
NASP shall establish and maintain the rules, standards, and policies governing the program and all NASP-sanctioned activities.

### 1.5.2.
NASP shall define and administer the official NASP curriculum, instructor training programs, equipment standards, and range protocols used in participating schools.

### 1.5.3.
NASP shall recognize and support State NASP Programs responsible for administering the program within their respective jurisdictions.

### 1.5.4.
NASP shall establish and sanction Local, State, National, and World competitions conducted under NASP rules.

### 1.5.5.
NASP shall establish, confirm, and maintain official NASP records, including international, national, and state records.

### 1.5.6.
NASP shall maintain official results from NASP-sanctioned competitions.

### 1.5.7.
NASP shall interpret and enforce NASP rules and shall have the authority to issue rulings on matters not otherwise addressed within the rules.

### 1.5.8.
NASP shall take appropriate action to protect the integrity of NASP competitions and activities.

## 1.6. Governance

### 1.6.1.
The governance and administration of NASP shall be vested in the NASP Board of Directors and the officers and administrative staff appointed to carry out the operations of the program.

### 1.6.2.
The NASP Board of Directors shall serve as the governing body of the organization and shall be responsible for the oversight of NASP policies, strategic direction, and program administration.

### 1.6.3.
The Board of Directors shall consist of five members.

### 1.6.4.
The officers of NASP shall include a President and a Vice President who shall provide leadership and oversight of the organization and its activities.

### 1.6.5.
The administration of NASP shall be supported by designated staff responsible for the management and operation of the program. These positions may include, but are not limited to:

* Director of Accounting and Financial Reporting

* Director of Training and Technology

* Project Director

* Business Office Manager

* Program Manager

### 1.6.6.
The officers and administrative staff of NASP shall carry out the duties necessary for the operation and administration of the program in accordance with the policies and direction established by the Board of Directors.

## 1.7. Authority and Interpretation of Rules

### 1.7.1.
The NASP Rules shall govern all NASP-sanctioned activities, including instruction, competitions, and events conducted under the authority of NASP.

### 1.7.2.
NASP shall have the authority to interpret and apply the NASP Rules and to issue rulings on matters arising from their application.

### 1.7.3.
In circumstances not expressly addressed by the NASP Rules, NASP officials or designated authorities may make determinations necessary for the administration of the program and the conduct of NASP activities.

### 1.7.4.
Such determinations shall be consistent with the objectives and standards of NASP and shall be considered authoritative for the matter in question.

## 1.8. Amendments

### 1.8.1.
The NASP Constitution and NASP Rules may be amended by the NASP Board of Directors.

### 1.8.2.
Amendments may be adopted when deemed necessary for the proper administration, development, or integrity of the program.

### 1.8.3.
All adopted amendments shall become effective on the date established by NASP.

### 1.8.4.
Amendments to the NASP Constitution and NASP Rules shall be published as part of the official NASP rules and documentation.
